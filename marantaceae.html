Marantaceae

What exactly is a marantaceae?

Marantaceae is a family of plants that encompasses many different species, including Calatheas, Marantas, Stromanthes, and Ctenanthes.

These plants are more commonly referred to as 'prayer plants' because at night they lift their leaves and lower them in the morning. It's very cute.

In my experience, these guys are a little (read: a lot) needier than other plants, (especially calatheas). Prayer plants are definitely not at the top of my list when I'm recommending plants to a beginner. They need a LOT of humidity to be happy - that's probably the biggest deal for these plants, and if they're getting enough humidity, they may not be as picky about other things such as watering or sunlight (but that's just in my experience).

What they lack in simplicity that make up in their beauty. The most beautiful plants I've ever seen are usually prayer plants.

Calathea Vittata

Ok people rag on Calatheas because they are total divas, but this one is doing great. She doesn't grow a ton, but that's okay.

Rating: 7/10

Calathea Beauty Star

Another healthy Calathea. These leaves are so pretty I cannot believe nature created this on its own. I've heard that this is the most easy-going calathea, and in my experience that's been true, so if you've done really well with some easier plants and want to dive into something a little more challenging, I'd probably recommend this one.

Rating: 8/10
